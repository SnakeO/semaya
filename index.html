Complaint for Damages
1. Plaintiff John Doe ("Plaintiff") brings this action against Defendants Semaya Beach Resort, Andika Putra, Sindra Sindrawan, and Does 1-10 (collectively "Defendants") for damages arising from Defendants' gross negligence, breach of contract, fraud, and willful misconduct that resulted in serious bodily injury, emotional distress, and financial harm to Plaintiff during his stay at Defendants' resort facility in Nusa Penida, Bali, Indonesia.
2. This Court has jurisdiction over this matter pursuant to 28 U.S.C. ยง 1332 as there is complete diversity of citizenship between the parties and the amount in controversy exceeds $75,000. Venue is proper in this District pursuant to 28 U.S.C. ยง 1391.
3. Plaintiff is a citizen and resident of the United States who was a paying guest at Defendants' resort facility from approximately [DATE] to [DATE], 2024.
4. Defendant Semaya Beach Resort is an Indonesian corporation operating a hospitality business at Jl. Raya Batukandik, Nusa Penida, Klungkung Regency, Bali 80771, Indonesia, marketing itself as a premium five-star resort facility.
5. Defendant Andika Putra is an individual who, at all times relevant herein, acted as Restaurant Manager for Defendant Semaya Beach Resort and was acting within the course and scope of his employment and/or agency relationship with said Defendant.
6. Defendant Sindra Sindrawan is an individual who, at all times relevant herein, acted as Operations Manager for Defendant Semaya Beach Resort and was acting within the course and scope of his employment and/or agency relationship with said Defendant.
Statement of Facts
7. On or about [DATE], 2024, Plaintiff, relying on Defendants' representations that Semaya Beach Resort was a premium hospitality establishment, booked accommodations at said facility for multiple nights at a cost of approximately $[AMOUNT] per night.
8. Upon arrival, Defendants immediately breached their duty of care by failing to provide basic amenities standard in the hospitality industry, including complimentary drinking water, which is considered essential for guest health and safety, particularly in tropical climates.
9. When Plaintiff requested drinking water, Defendants attempted to charge the unconscionable sum of 160,000 Indonesian Rupees (approximately $10 USD) for two bottles of water, then subsequently replaced sealed, safe bottled water with unsanitary refillable containers of questionable origin and safety, as depicted in Exhibit A attached hereto and incorporated herein by reference.
10. On or about [DATE], 2024, Plaintiff and his companion ordered rare beef at Defendants' restaurant facility. Instead of the ordered beef, Defendants served Plaintiff raw chicken or other poultry, creating an immediate and serious health hazard.
11. The meat served to Plaintiff was visibly and texturally inconsistent with beef, as demonstrated by the photographic evidence attached hereto as Exhibit B and Exhibit C.
12. When Plaintiff complained about the obviously incorrect and dangerous food service, Defendant Andika Putra knowingly and willfully made false representations to Plaintiff, insisting multiple times that the raw poultry was beef, despite clear evidence to the contrary.
13. Plaintiff, seeking independent verification of his observations, consulted with a professional chef who confirmed that the substance served was not beef, as evidenced by the communications attached hereto as Exhibit D.
14. Plaintiff further sought verification through online forums dedicated to steak identification, where multiple experts confirmed the served substance was not beef. Plaintiff was ultimately banned from said forum for posting images of non-beef products, as shown in Exhibit E.
15. As a direct and proximate result of consuming the contaminated raw poultry served by Defendants, Plaintiff and his companion suffered severe gastrointestinal illness, stomach cramps, and other symptoms consistent with foodborne illness.
16. Fearing parasitic infection from the unknown raw meat consumption, Plaintiff was forced to seek medical attention and consume anti-parasitic medications as a precautionary measure, as evidenced by Exhibit F and Exhibit G attached hereto.
17. Despite being directly responsible for the contaminated food service, Defendants refused to pay for Plaintiff's necessary medical treatment, adding insult to injury and demonstrating a callous disregard for guest welfare.
18. Defendant Sindra Sindrawan initially acknowledged to Plaintiff that the served substance was not beef, but later, under apparent pressure from other Defendants, changed his story and participated in the cover-up by falsely claiming the dangerous substance was beef.
19. To further deceive Plaintiff, Defendants showed packaging of actual beef as "proof" of what was served, despite clear evidence that the actual served product was entirely different, as shown in Exhibit H.
20. When challenged, Defendants cooked actual beef in their kitchen, which bore no resemblance to the raw poultry originally served to Plaintiff, as demonstrated by the comparative evidence in Exhibit I.
21. Artificial intelligence analysis confirmed Plaintiff's observations, identifying the properly cooked beef as genuine beef while determining that the original served substance could not be beef due to incorrect color and texture, as evidenced in Exhibit J and Exhibit K.
22. Defendants' facility contained numerous additional health and safety violations, including contaminated water systems producing yellow-colored water unfit for human consumption, as demonstrated in Exhibit L (video evidence).
23. The accommodations featured inadequate bathroom facilities with no privacy and cross-contamination from cigarette smoke due to poor architectural design, creating additional health hazards as shown in Exhibit M and Exhibit N.
24. Defendants' housekeeping staff violated basic sanitation protocols by cleaning guest rooms while wearing outdoor footwear, thereby tracking external contaminants into supposedly clean accommodations, as evidenced in Exhibit O.
25. Defendants provided dangerous and defective recreational equipment, including bicycles with defective brakes, flat tires, and broken components that posed serious safety risks to guests, as shown in Exhibit P and video evidence Exhibit Q.
26. The facility's advertised fitness amenities consisted of broken, unstable equipment unsuitable for guest use, constituting false advertising and breach of promised services, as documented in Exhibit R and Exhibit S (video evidence).
27. Even basic amenities provided by Defendants were defective, including a wooden comb that broke during normal use, symbolically representing the overall failure of Defendants' operations, as shown in Exhibit T.
First Cause of Action
(Negligence)
28. Plaintiff incorporates by reference each and every allegation contained in paragraphs 1 through 27, inclusive, as though fully set forth herein.
29. Defendants owed Plaintiff a duty of care to provide safe accommodations, food, and services consistent with industry standards for hospitality establishments.
30. Defendants breached their duty of care by, among other things: (a) serving contaminated and dangerous raw poultry instead of properly prepared beef; (b) failing to maintain sanitary food preparation and service standards; (c) providing contaminated water systems; (d) maintaining unsafe and defective recreational equipment; (e) failing to ensure adequate sanitation protocols; and (f) providing substandard accommodations.
31. As a direct and proximate result of Defendants' negligence, Plaintiff suffered bodily injury, illness, medical expenses, pain and suffering, and other damages in an amount to be proven at trial.
Second Cause of Action
(Breach of Contract)
32. Plaintiff incorporates by reference each and every allegation contained in paragraphs 1 through 31, inclusive, as though fully set forth herein.
33. Plaintiff and Defendants entered into a contract whereby Defendants agreed to provide premium hospitality services, accommodations, food service, and amenities in exchange for payment.
34. Plaintiff performed all conditions precedent to performance by Defendants, including payment of all required fees.
35. Defendants materially breached the contract by failing to provide services consistent with their representations and industry standards, instead providing dangerous, substandard, and defective services.
36. As a direct result of Defendants' breach, Plaintiff suffered damages including but not limited to bodily injury, medical expenses, loss of enjoyment of vacation, and other damages to be proven at trial.
Third Cause of Action
(Fraud and Misrepresentation)
37. Plaintiff incorporates by reference each and every allegation contained in paragraphs 1 through 36, inclusive, as though fully set forth herein.
38. Defendants made material misrepresentations of fact to Plaintiff, including but not limited to: (a) falsely representing that raw poultry was beef; (b) misrepresenting the quality and safety of their food services; (c) falsely advertising premium hospitality services; and (d) concealing known health and safety hazards.
39. Defendants knew such representations were false or made them with reckless disregard for their truth, particularly when Defendant Andika Putra repeatedly insisted that raw poultry was beef despite obvious evidence to the contrary.
40. Defendants made these misrepresentations with the intent to deceive Plaintiff and avoid responsibility for their negligent and dangerous conduct.
41. Plaintiff reasonably relied on Defendants' misrepresentations to his detriment, suffering damages as alleged herein.
Fourth Cause of Action
(Intentional Infliction of Emotional Distress)
42. Plaintiff incorporates by reference each and every allegation contained in paragraphs 1 through 41, inclusive, as though fully set forth herein.
43. Defendants' conduct was extreme and outrageous, including serving contaminated raw poultry to guests, lying about the nature of the food served, attempting to gaslight Plaintiff about his own observations, and refusing to take responsibility for clear health and safety violations.
44. Defendants intended to cause emotional distress or acted with reckless disregard for the probability of causing emotional distress through their deceptive and dangerous conduct.
45. As a direct result of Defendants' extreme and outrageous conduct, Plaintiff suffered severe emotional distress, anxiety, fear for his health and safety, and other psychological damages.
Wherefore, Plaintiff prays for judgment against Defendants as follows:
A. For compensatory damages in an amount to be proven at trial, but not less than $100,000;
B. For punitive damages in an amount sufficient to punish Defendants and deter similar conduct;
C. For medical expenses incurred as a result of Defendants' conduct;
D. For pain and suffering damages;
E. For emotional distress damages;
F. For costs of suit and reasonable attorneys' fees;
G. For pre and post-judgment interest at the legal rate; and
H. For such other and further relief as this Court deems just and proper.
Plaintiff demands trial by jury on all issues so triable.
