üíß Red Flag #1: The Water Bottle Scam

Okay babes, let me start with something that should be basic hospitality 101. I get to my room, unpack my cute travel outfits, and look around for complimentary water bottles. Nothing. Nada. Not even a sad little plastic cup.

I call the front desk thinking there's been some mistake. "Hi, could I get some water bottles for my room please?"

Twenty minutes later, housekeeping arrives with two sealed bottles and a bill for 160,000 Indonesian Rupees - that's about $10 USD for two bottles of water! üò±

üö® Plot Twist Alert!

When I complained about the outrageous charge, management admitted they "should have" provided free water. But instead of giving me proper sealed bottles, they took away the ones I'd paid for and replaced them with sketchy refillable containers!

Y'all, this is Indonesia. Water safety is already a concern, and now they're giving me mysterious liquid in random containers? My travel anxiety was through the roof!

Zero Privacy When Nature Calls

The bathroom had this "open-air" design that might look cute on Pinterest, but becomes a nightmare when you're dealing with food poisoning. My neighbors could hear EVERYTHING, and I could hear their conversations from the toilet.

Plus, they put smoking and non-smoking rooms right next to each other, so cigarette smoke constantly drifted into our space. Not exactly the zen retreat vibes I was going for!

The roads in Nusa Penida have zero lighting at night. These bikes could have literally killed me!
